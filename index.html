Sharpened Voice

A grounding ritual for speaking

The Sharpened Voice is an instruction-based participatory object that connects the act of speaking to a ritual of sharpening a pencil and archiving it to show what was shed in order to speak. Designed to help people feel calmer and more present while speaking in group discussions.

Before speaking, participants may sharpen a pencil. They begin speaking while sharpening. The motion regulates breath, slows the body, and transforms nervous energy into focus.

How It Works

1. Pick up a pencil.
2. Begin sharpening.
3. Speak while sharpening.
4. Place the shavings in a clear container.

Participation is optional. The ritual is meant to support, not control, conversation.
